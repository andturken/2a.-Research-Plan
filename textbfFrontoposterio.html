Fronto-posterior fiber tract disconnection common to post-stroke ideomotor apraxia and aphasia. A classical observation in behavioral neurology is the common occurrence of apraxia and aphasia. We investigated the common anatomical basis for these two brain disorders in a VLSM study of 108 chronic stroke patients [26]. Results suggested that the disconnection of the AF/SLF complex underlies both ideomotor apraxia and aphasia (Figure 8). This finding is consistent with a network perspective of high-level functions, as ideomotor apraxia can be characterized as a failure to translate action representations into movements, and severe aphasia as a failure to transform language representations into speech.

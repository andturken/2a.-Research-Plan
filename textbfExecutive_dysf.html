Executive dysfunction and the left superior longitudinal fasciculus. Executive function is critical for coping for everyday challenges, and thus for stroke functional outcome. We analyzed Wisconsin Card Sorting task performance [WCST, 60] in 51 left-hemisphere chronic stroke patients using VLSM and tract lesion load analysis, and found that revealed that both left frontal and posterior (temporo-parietal) lesions affect set shifting performance, as well as white matter lesions along the superior longitudinal fasciculus (Figure 7). These findings implicated fronto-temporo-parietal disconnection in executive dysfunction.
